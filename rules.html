Article 03

Roster Construction

Each team fields a 19-slot roster: 10 starters, 7 bench spots, and 2 IR slots.

QB WR WR RB RB TE W/R/T FLEX W/R/T FLEX K DEF BN BN BN BN BN BN BN IR IR
  • The two FLEX slots accept WR, RB, or TE — any combination is allowed.
  • IR slots are available for players with an official Out or Doubtful designation. Players with lesser injury statuses such as Questionable are not eligible for IR.
  • Lineup changes are permitted up until the individual player's game kicks off.
  • Injured players may not be added directly from waivers into an IR slot — they must be rostered normally first, then moved to IR.

  • All trades are subject to commissioner review. The commissioner may veto any trade deemed collusive or grossly unbalanced.
  • Draft pick trades are not permitted in this league.
  • When a player is dropped, they are placed on waivers for 2 days before becoming a free agent. Any team can put in a waiver claim during that window.
  • Waiver priority is a continual rolling list — the team that makes a successful claim moves to the bottom of the priority order.
  • Waivers process every Wednesday at 3 AM EDT. Players are locked upon the start of their week's game.

  • The top 7 teams by regular-season record qualify for the championship playoff bracket. In the event of a tie, the tiebreaker is best record against the tied opponent(s).
  • Teams ranked 8th through 12th enter a consolation bracket running simultaneously during Weeks 15–17.
  • Playoff bracket seeding is set at the start of Week 15 and does not reseed between rounds.
  • Teams eliminated from playoff contention have their rosters locked — they cannot make adds, drops, or trades.
